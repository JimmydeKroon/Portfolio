Breakfast app

Breakfast-app is a project I made to practice my React skills and I decided to use nextjs because of how easy it is to setup + the features it offers out of the box. Inspiration for this app came from the fact that I always skip breakfast even though I know it is wrong, the app will offer people a subscription based breakfast service so you always have breakfast without thinking about it.

Visit live link!

The app is fairly basic in the current state, but all of the features are custom made. The app uses the context react API to store the current cart items in a global state, this way i can acces this information on any page and in any component. The cart information is also stored in localstorage to make sure information is saved for future visits.


The menu has a filter feature that functions thanks to a react state.

After adding an item to the cart an animation will be played in the menu on the left to change the number, indicating that an item was added. The animation is tracked by another state to monitor when an animation has ended so it can be played again on future clicks.

The cart page shows all the current items added to the cart. The total price is updated dynamically thanks to react when deleting or adding items (next on the to-do list: adding the price of the product multiple times when the quantity is more than 1)

Adding or removing items will update the global state and after that the localstorage, so the information is always up to date.

Principle

Music app concept

For my graduation project I made this music inspiration app. It helps producers quickly store ideas on their phone by humming or beatboxing, the app turns these audio recordings into midi notes!

Check out the promo video here! -->

The idea for this app came from personal interest. When I started learning music production, one of the problems I ran into was remembering some of my ideas and accurately recreating my ideas on the computer.

The main feature of this app is turning audio into midi, this feature already exists in multiple music production software. When users get some inspiration they can use the app to record their humming, beatboxing or singing. These recordings can then be turned into musical notes. After this the notes can be played by any virtual instrument, giving musicians a new way to quickly sample ideas!


Here (on the left) you can see the recording process, users can just push the button and start recording. Afterwards there is an option to save or start editing.

On the right you can see the result. The play button allows users to listen to the recording. Extra options will appear after clicking on the box, "convert to midi" will transform the recording into notes.


Now that the recording has been transformed into notes, an instrument can be added. The selected instrument will play the notes generated from the recording and users can change the instrument to find out what works best.

A new sound can be added to a project at any time, this will add a new underneath the last one. This new recording can play another instrument after converting to midi, or if it is singing you can just leave it. All recordings can be played together by using the play all button for unique and fun results!

Logodrops

In 2017 I started working on a online logo generator with a friend. We wanted to make logos easily accesible and affordable for everyone, because most of the time people don't have the connections or the money to hire a designer. Logodrops gives users an endless amount of unique logos by simply anwsering a few questions.

VISIT LOGODROPS!

For this project I worked specifically on the overall design and the User experience & user interaction. Logodrops has a lot of pages, buttons, tools and features so making all of this work well together was the hard part. After my design work I started helping out with the front end.

Logodrops has 3 easy steps. You start with a company name, then you can choose a style(s), a color(s) and you can search for an icon. After making your choices Logodrops can generate and almost unlimited amount of logos. Create and account to save favorites and keep track of bought logos, or even make adjustments to bought logos.


The first step asks the user to pick one or multiple styles. These will affect the overall style of the logo & letters. You can select as many styles as you want. After selecting at least 1, you can click on continue to go to the next step.


In the second step users can choose a color for their logo, up to 3 different colors can be selected. Users can also enter a specific color by using the plus sign.


In the 3rd and final step users can pick an icon to complete their logo. We use a large library of icons (2 million+) to give users plenty of choice. We ask users to describe their business in one word to help them find the right icons. You can pick a total of 5 icons.


Logodrops will mix all of the ingredients together and make a bunch of logos to choose from. On the bottom of the page you can load more if you want. From this screen users can create an account and finalyze the purchase if they are happy.


Making Logodrops was not an easy task, a lot of UI/UX design tweaking and adjusting was needed to fit in all of the features. We are still working on improving and expanding Logodrops, making it even better than it already is.

Beatogether step sequencer webapp


Beatogether is a webapp I made that allows users to create beats. It is based on how a basic step sequencer works. Users can enable specific sounds by clicking on the squares, pressing play will the loop from left to right and play all the sqaures that are enabled.

The app uses websocket technology, allowing users to create beats together. Every person connecting to the website will have a live connection to all other users, so when one person switches one of the squares on or off all other users will see this change!


In order to coordinate I added a chat, it has some cool little features like unique colors per user and showing your own messages to the right & in orange and works with websockets aswell.

The app also syncs changes to a database so all changes are kept up to date with all users, I used google's Firebase for this.


The app is really fun to play with so I suggest trying it out yourself! (also try connecting with multiple people/computers to see the websockets in action!)

F1 live stats webapp

A webapp built to show live stats from the current F1 race for people that can't watch live. It shows all the drivers in their current position, the tyres and their lap times.

The app has 3 pages. The homescreen shows all 20 drivers and their stats, users can pick their favorite drivers by clicking on the heart icon.


The "mydrivers" page will only show the chosen drivers and their information. Changes in the data will make the page update in realtime and will trigger a notification from the browser about the update.


Unfortunately Formula 1 does not have an open API with the data I want, so I faked it for this project. The data is stored in a MongoDB database and it can be manipulated from an admin page. Changing values on this screen will trigger changes and notification on the user pages, this is done with a simple html form.

Cool extra info: the app works with and without javascipt! if javascript is enabled the update will use ajax to complete the changes on the page itself. If javascript is disabled the page will be refreshed to see the changes.

The next Spacex launch

I have been following Spacex for a while now, and watching the launches live on youtube is always fun. But I don't know when most of the launches are and I end up missing most of them. There is a really good unofficial Spacex API that has a lot of information about every single launch by Spacex (past, present & future!), so I decided to make a simple countdown timer to see when the next Spacex launch is with some extra information about the mission.

Check the unofficial Spacex API github page below!

API Github page


The homepage shows a countdown to the next Spacex launch, this could be a cargo mission or a mission with astronauts to the ISS for example. The date is fetched with an API call and using the current time from the computer the remaining time until launch is calculated, the numbers then get updated every second.

The page has direct links to the youtube livestream, reddit campaign and wiki page for this mission. If you want to know more about the mission you can click on "see launch details".


The details page shows a description about the mission, the launchpad being used and the payload. The payload changes depending on it being supplies or people (Spacex does not have a lot of crew flights yet).

A task board for someone with specific disabilities.

Figma

Alternative task board

Marijn is a software developer with a specific disabilities which makes it hard for him to use a computer. He can't really move his fingers and his hands stay in the same position, he has learned how to use his computer in a very unique way. One thing he still struggles with sometimes is his trello board, because he has to move certain items from one side of his screen all the way to the other side. This project is an experiment to see if I can make this task easier for him. The end result was made with HTML, CSS and Javascript.

Try it out yourself!

Task board live link


The board looks like any other task board but works a little different. Ater experimenting with drag & drop for a while I realized Marijn did not have a lot of trouble dragging and dropping, but it could definitely be made easier. I decided I wanted to make the distance Marijn had to move across his trackpad shorter.


The result is this functionality where Marijn can click on a certain task and a submenu will pop up. This menu represents the other boards and clicking one of them will then move this task to that board. While hovering over this menu the boards will light up indicating where the task is going to end up.

Marijn had a lot of fun trying out the app (and trying the break the app, he is a developer) but had 1 important question. What if there are more boards? The menu might not have enough space...


Unfortunately I did not have enough time to code the solution with javascript, but I did make a design in figma. The core works the same except for the fact that now the app uses a wheel instead of text. When you hover over a certain board that part of the wheel will become larger so you can see better. In case Marijn prefers to use his keys he can use K and J to go back and forth between the wheel items!
